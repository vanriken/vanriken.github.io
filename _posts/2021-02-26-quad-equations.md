A quadratic equation is a polynomial equation of the form 

$$ \alpha x^2 + \beta x + \gamma = 0. $$

The roots of this equation are given by

$$ x_{1,2} = \frac{-\beta}{2\alpha} \pm \frac{\sqrt{\Delta}}{2\alpha}, $$ 

where the determinant $\Delta$ is equal to 

$$ \Delta = \beta^2 - 4\alpha\gamma. $$

This approach undoubtetly works but it may be tricky to remember the equations that are needed. Luckily, it is possible to derive the roots of the quadratic equation in a way that is much more intuitive.
